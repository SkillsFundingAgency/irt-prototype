Who is the course for:

This course gives you the skills you need to work in jobs such as nursing,social work and residential care.It’s also a route into a degree programme in health and social care.Taking in everything from human anatomy and physiology to equality and diversity at work,this Extended Diploma will also give you valuable work experience.

What you’ll learn:

You'll learn about topics such as public health, sociological and psychological perspectives, caring for children, and young people, and safeguarding adults and promoting independence. You'll also study:effective communication equality diversity and rights anatomy and physiology social sciences

How you’ll learn

Most of the course is taught in the classroom,but you’ll do work placements throughout the programme,completing up to 100 hours in a variety of health and social care settings over the year.You’ll also complete a research project.

Where next?

If you pass with merit or distinction,a BTEC Extended Diploma is the equivalent of three A levels,and opens up the opportunity for you to study health,social sciences or a related field at university.It’s a well-respected qualification in business,so it should help you progress within your workplace,particularly in areas such as human resources.
